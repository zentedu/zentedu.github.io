Kyoto

Book

Kyoto is considered the cultural capital of Japan and is a major tourist destination. It is home to many Buddhist temples, Shinto shrines, palaces and gardens, many of which are recognized by UNESCO as World Heritage Sites. Famous landmarks include Kyoto Imperial Palace, Kiyomizu-dera, Kinkaku-ji, Ginkaku-ji and Katsura Imperial Villa. Kyoto is also a center for postgraduate training, with Kyoto University being a world-renowned school.

Hiroshima

The city rose from the ashes...read more

Book

Hiroshima Japan is one of the prefectures that suffered the heaviest damage after the second world war. Hiroshima City is one of two cities that suffered an atomic bomb that killed about 140,000 people, many injured, and more than 60% of its architecture destroyed. After the war, the Japanese joined hands to rebuild Hiroshima from the ruins and now peace has returned, Hiroshima also wears a new "shirt color" no less than the most developed city in Japan. Remnants of war are still remembered by the people of Hiroshima with the Peace Memorial - a symbol of peace in Hiroshima.
